Sikkim - Cultural Heritage

About Sikkim

Sikkim, known for its breathtaking landscapes and rich cultural heritage, is famous for its diverse flora and fauna, traditional crafts, and festivals. The state reflects a blend of various cultures and traditions.

Notable Heritage Sites

  • Gangtok: The capital city known for its stunning views of the Himalayas and rich cultural heritage.
  • Rumtek Monastery: A significant Buddhist monastery known for its beautiful architecture and serene ambiance.
  • Khangchendzonga National Park: A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its stunning natural beauty and biodiversity.

Traditional Cuisine

Sikkim's culinary heritage is diverse and flavorful, with dishes like Momos, Thukpa, and Gundruk being popular among locals and visitors alike.

Arts and Crafts

The state is known for its vibrant handicrafts, including handwoven carpets, traditional masks, and wooden carvings, reflecting the rich cultural tapestry of Sikkim.
